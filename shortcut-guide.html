Create Your CalScan Shortcut

Set up a one-tap workflow on your iPhone to scan documents and add events to your calendar

📋 Prerequisites
  • iPhone running iOS 15 or later
  • Shortcuts app installed (comes pre-installed)
  • Camera access enabled for photo scanning

Step-by-Step Guide

1 Open Shortcuts App 📱

Open the Shortcuts app on your iPhone. It has a blue and pink icon with overlapping squares.

Can't find it? Swipe down on your home screen and search for "Shortcuts"

2 Create New Shortcut ➕

Tap the "+" button in the top-right corner to create a new shortcut.

You'll see a blank shortcut editor ready for actions.

3 Add Photo Action 📸

Tap "Add Action" and search for "Take Photo" or "Select Photos".

Choose "Take Photo" to capture documents with your camera, or "Select Photos" to pick from your library.

Tip: "Take Photo" is faster for scanning documents on the go

4 Add "Open URL" Action 🌐

Search for the "Open URLs" action and add it below the photo action.

In the URL field, enter the web address where CalScan is hosted (or your local URL).

This will open CalScan in Safari where you can process the captured photo

5 Name Your Shortcut ✏️

Tap the dropdown arrow ▼ at the top of the editor and select "Rename".

Name it "CalScan" or "Scan Events".

Tap "Choose Icon" and pick a calendar emoji 📅 for easy recognition.

6 Add to Home Screen 🏠

Tap the dropdown arrow ▼ again and select "Add to Home Screen".

This creates an app-like icon on your home screen for one-tap access!

Your CalScan shortcut will appear alongside your other apps

⚡ Even Easier: Use CalScan as a Web App

You don't even need a Shortcut! Just add CalScan directly to your Home Screen:

  1. Open CalScan in Safari on your iPhone
  2. Tap the Share button (the square with an arrow pointing up)
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. CalScan will work like a native app — no App Store needed!
💡 When you download the .ics file, Safari will automatically open the native "Add to Calendar" dialog — no extra steps needed.
💡 Tips for Best Results
📐 Hold your phone parallel to the document for a straight capture
💡 Ensure good lighting — avoid shadows and glare on the document
🔍 Make sure text is sharp and in focus before capturing
📄 For multi-page documents, use PDF format for best results
🗓️ Standard date formats work best — e.g., "June 15, 2026" or "15/06/2026"
🕐 Include times when possible — e.g., "Meeting at 3:00 PM"
